Creative Idea

The project does not simply label slow traffic. Its central idea is speed deviation: comparing a zone-hour's current average speed against its own historical baseline. This helps separate real congestion events from zones that are naturally slow because of geography, roads, or local conditions.

Pipeline

  • Data LoadingIngests raw NYC TLC Yellow Taxi parquet files for six months of trips.
  • PreprocessingApplies quality filters for timestamps, distance, duration, speed, zones, duplicates, and optional per-zone IQR outlier handling.
  • Feature EngineeringBuilds time features, speed_mph, zone-hour aggregation, cyclic hour encoding, log trip density, and the speed_deviation feature.
  • ClusteringUses scaling, elbow selection, K-Means clustering, optional DBSCAN hotspot detection, and cluster-quality evaluation.
  • VisualizationPrecomputes dashboard data, metrics, static diagnostic charts, and interactive Streamlit views.

Dashboard Experience

The dashboard lets users filter by hour, day type, borough, and cluster focus. It shows a Pydeck map of congestion zones, KPI cards, a generated story summary, cluster legend, hourly trend, centroid radar, heatmap, top congested zones, diagnostics, and an expandable raw-data table.

Outcome

  • Built a full offline-to-online analytics system with pipeline stages, cached artifacts, app pages, tests, Docker, and Makefile shortcuts.
  • Converted millions of taxi trips into zone-hour observations that can be explored quickly in Streamlit.
  • Added model diagnostics including Silhouette, Davies-Bouldin, Calinski-Harabasz, per-cluster silhouette, seed-stability analysis, and visual charts.
